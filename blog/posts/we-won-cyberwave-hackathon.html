We Won the Cyberwave Hackathon

March 2026 · 4 min read

Shivam and I won the Cyberwave hackathon this weekend, and I’m still a little shocked.

The app we built is for frontline workers. If you’ve ever worked retail or hospitality, you know that no one really prepares you for the hard parts of the job. You get thrown into situations with angry, hostile customers and you’re just supposed to figure it out. We wanted to change that.

The idea is simple: you open the app, and you can either prep for an upcoming job interview or jump straight into a simulated hostile customer interaction. The simulation puts you in a real conversation with a difficult customer, and you have to work through it in real time. When it’s over, the app grades you on how well you de‑escalated the situation and helped resolve things. You get actual feedback, not just a thumbs up or down.

The grading part was what I was most proud of. It’s one thing to simulate a tough conversation. It’s another to tell someone what they did well and what they could have handled differently. That’s the part that actually makes it useful.

Hackathons are a weird kind of fun. You’re under pressure, making fast decisions, cutting scope constantly. Shivam and I had good chemistry and just kept building. By the time we submitted, we had something that felt real and purposeful.

Winning was a bonus. Building something that could genuinely help someone walk into their first shift a little more prepared? That’s the part that stuck.
