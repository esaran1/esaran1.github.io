MIT Zero Robotics Finals: 3rd Place Internationally (Alliance 10)

March 2026 · 4 min read

I'm still kind of processing this.

MIT Zero Robotics is a competition where student teams write code to control simulated satellites competing in a zero-gravity environment. The finals pit the best teams from around the world against each other, and the stakes feel very real.

It took months of work. A lot of things didn't work, got scrapped, and got rebuilt. But we figured it out.

When the results came in and we landed 3rd place internationally, it didn't sink in at first. Out of all the teams from around the world, we were on the podium.

Beyond the placement, what sticks with me is what the competition demanded: systems thinking, teamwork under pressure, and the humbling experience of having your work tested in a setting where it has to work the first time, every time.

More motivated than ever. Onward.
