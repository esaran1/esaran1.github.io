We Won First Place at GASTC with Cricket Coach

March 2026 · 5 min read

Shivam and I just took first place at the Georgia Student Technology Competition, and it feels really good.

The project is called Cricket Coach. The core idea: cricket players, especially at the amateur level, don't have easy access to real coaching feedback. You can record yourself batting or bowling, but watching the video back doesn't tell you much unless you know what to look for. We wanted to change that.

You upload a short clip, pick your camera angle and mode (batting or bowling), and the app does the rest. On the backend, we run pose estimation frame by frame using MediaPipe, then convert those body landmarks into actual coaching metrics like joint angles, alignment, and movement stability. Each metric gets benchmarked and labeled as good, okay, or needs work. You get an overlay video with guidance drawn on top, a structured report, and coaching tips filtered by what actually needs attention.

A few things we built that we're especially proud of. One is the kinematic sequence analysis, which tracks how power moves through the body from the pelvis up through the torso, shoulder, and wrist. If the sequence is out of order, the app flags it as a power leak and explains why that matters. Another is the pro comparison feature, which uses Dynamic Time Warping to align your movement sequence with a selected pro player's, then renders a ghost overlay so you can see exactly where your mechanics differ. We also built bowling injury risk detection that catches mixed action patterns, which is a real mechanical issue that can lead to injury over time.

On the frontend we used Next.js and Recharts for the results interface, and we added Hindi language support to make it more accessible to players who need it.

GASTC was a great experience. Competing in front of judges and having to explain not just what it does but why the technical decisions matter pushed us to think more clearly about the project. First place was the outcome, but building something with this much substance was the real win.
