ClearText: Evidence-Constrained, Local-First Document Intelligence

Project overview

ClearText is a local-first, production-oriented AI system designed to improve access to government and public-service programs by transforming complex official documents into clear, actionable, and verifiable guidance. The project addresses a persistent challenge I observed in my community: individuals are often eligible for critical public services but are effectively excluded because the documents explaining how to apply are dense, unstructured, and difficult to interpret.

I first encountered this issue while volunteering at my local public library, where staff routinely assist patrons with benefit, financial-aid, and enrollment paperwork but lack the capacity to support everyone who needs help. These documents are typically written in legal or bureaucratic language at a high reading level, creating systemic barriers for first-generation families, non-native English speakers, elderly residents, and students navigating these processes independently. As a result, applications are frequently delayed, submitted incorrectly, or abandoned altogether.

ClearText is designed as an assistive system that improves comprehension without replacing human judgment or institutional authority. Its beneficiaries include community members seeking access to public services as well as the librarians, school counselors, and community organizations that support them. By converting opaque documents into structured, plain-language workflows, ClearText helps reduce friction in public-service access, improve equity, and scale limited human support resources.

From a systems perspective, ClearText is implemented as a modular web platform with a Python FastAPI backend and a modern frontend built using Next.js with TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, and shadcn-style UI components. The architecture is intentionally local-first and CPU-compatible, enabling deployment in environments such as libraries, schools, and community centers without reliance on cloud infrastructure or proprietary APIs. Document ingestion supports both plain text and PDF formats, with optional OCR using Tesseract for scanned or image-heavy forms.

The AI layer is built around a constrained natural language processing pipeline that prioritizes reliability, traceability, and explicit uncertainty handling. Rather than using free-form text generation, ClearText employs a two-stage, evidence-first approach. In the first stage, the system performs structured extraction of key “Form Facts,” including eligibility criteria, deadlines, required documents, fees, submission steps, contact information, and warnings. This extraction relies on a combination of spaCy-based NLP, regular expressions, and heuristic rules, producing structured outputs that are directly grounded in the source document. Each extracted element is linked to exact quoted text and character offsets, creating a verifiable chain between input and output.

In the second stage, ClearText generates user-facing explanations such as overviews, guided checklists, common pitfalls, and question-and-answer responses exclusively from the validated extracted facts. The system enforces strict refusal behavior: if a user asks for information that is not present in the document, ClearText responds with “Not specified in the document,” rather than speculating. When configured, a small open-weight local language model can assist with proposing structured facts via llama.cpp, but any claim without verifiable textual evidence is automatically discarded. For long documents, extraction is performed in chunks to avoid context limitations while preserving grounding guarantees.

One of the central challenges during development was mitigating fluent but incorrect AI output. Early prototypes that relied on unconstrained summarization produced explanations that appeared helpful but occasionally introduced unsupported details. Identifying this failure mode led to a fundamental redesign of the system around separation of concerns: extraction, validation, and generation are isolated stages with explicit contracts between them. This redesign significantly reduced hallucinations and improved debuggability. Additional challenges included handling inconsistent document formatting and scanned PDFs, which were addressed through iterative heuristic refinement, chunked processing, and OCR integration.

ClearText represents a creative and responsible application of AI by treating constraints as a design principle rather than a limitation. Unlike general-purpose conversational AI systems optimized for fluency, ClearText optimizes for correctness, auditability, and user trust. Outputs are reorganized into a consistent, guided flow, covering eligibility, deadlines, required documents, fees, submission steps, contacts, and warnings, allowing users to quickly understand both what matters and what actions are required. The interface exposes citations directly, enabling users to verify each claim by jumping to the relevant section of the original document or viewing highlighted text spans.

Accuracy and reliability were evaluated using real public-service documents. Extracted deadlines, requirements, and submission steps were manually verified against source text, with ClearText correctly grounding over 90% of key elements on the first pass. The system was also tested using adversarial queries designed to induce hallucinations; in all cases, it correctly refused to generate unsupported information. These results demonstrated that the grounding and validation mechanisms functioned as intended.

Developing ClearText significantly deepened my understanding of how AI systems should be designed and deployed in high-stakes, real-world contexts. I learned that responsible AI is less about maximizing model capability and more about defining clear scope, enforcing safeguards, and making system limitations visible to users. This project reinforced the importance of designing AI that augments human understanding rather than replacing decision-making.

Beyond the core functionality, the system incorporates production-level considerations such as rate limiting, ephemeral document handling to protect privacy, and containerized deployment using Docker, with starter Kubernetes manifests for scalability. Overall, ClearText represents a complete technical and ethical system, demonstrating how AI can be thoughtfully engineered to address a real community problem while prioritizing trust, accessibility, and long-term viability.
